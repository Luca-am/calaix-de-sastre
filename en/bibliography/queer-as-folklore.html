Queer as Folklore

Sacha Coward

Queer as Folklore gathers legends and myths that mainstream historiography has either ignored or stripped of their queerness. Coward guides us through stories of pirates, ghosts, and sea creatures to show that queer identities and desires have always been present, even when the official record tried to erase them.

The book helps me look at heritage differently: it asks who tells the stories and who is left out when we repeat them uncritically. It is an act of reclamation that gives back a voice to characters who had been domesticated, inviting us to find models of care and dissent in the popular imagination.
